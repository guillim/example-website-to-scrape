About the Shop

Half a century of quietly recommending books to people who didn't know they needed them.

How we began

The Inkwell & Quill opened in the spring of 1972, in the rooms above what was then Hargreaves & Son, watchmakers. The shop's founder, Margaret “Maggie” Tilbrook, had spent twelve years cataloguing rare manuscripts at a university library three counties over before deciding she would rather sell paperbacks to schoolchildren and arguing about poetry with retired postal workers. She did both, with great success, for thirty-eight years.

Three rooms and a staircase

We moved down to the ground floor in 1988, after the watchmaker retired and let us have the lease. The shop today is three small interconnected rooms: the front room (new releases, periodicals, the till), the middle room (fiction, alphabetised by author, occasionally by mood), and the back room (non-fiction, children's, and the reading nook). A staircase leads to the loft, which is mostly cookery books and a great deal of dust.

What we believe

We believe that bookshops should be quiet but not silent, that recommendations should come with reasons, and that a well-chosen book given at the right moment can be one of the most generous things a person can offer another. We also believe that any cup of coffee priced above £3.20 is essentially a confidence trick, which is why ours costs £2.40 and comes with a small biscuit.

Our cat

Margaret is a tabby. She is twelve. She sleeps on the poetry shelf, specifically on the works of Mary Oliver, which we have come to regard as a form of editorial endorsement. She does not respond to her name unless food is involved.

The owners now

Since 2010 the shop has been owned by three former Inkwell booksellers — Marit Sølberg, Jaya Kapoor, and Theo Chen — who collectively have over sixty years behind the counter and roughly the same number of unread books on their respective bedside tables. They take it in turns to run things and have not yet had a serious argument about stock.

If you'd like to know more — or if you'd like a book you can't find anywhere else — please get in touch.
